What?

I joined a morning devotion organized by the Confederate Student Council of the College of Computer Studies (CCS) at my department on August 19 at Silliman Church. The activity aimed to bring students together for a moment of spiritual reflection and encouragement before starting the day. Along with my classmates, I participated in the session, which included a short message, prayer, and worship songs. The organizers led the devotion, ensuring a warm and welcoming environment for everyone.

So What?

Attending the devotion was a refreshing way to start the day and reminded me of the importance of balancing academics with spiritual well-being. Initially, I thought it would be a simple gathering, but I was pleasantly surprised by how uplifting and inspiring the session felt. One of the speakers left a strong impression on me with their heartfelt message about perseverance and faith. The most satisfying part was feeling a sense of unity with my classmates and department, which made the experience meaningful and motivating.

Now What?

This experience taught me the value of setting aside time for reflection and community-building, even in a busy college schedule. I realized how such gatherings could strengthen connections among students while providing spiritual guidance. Moving forward, I plan to attend more devotions and encourage others to join as well. I see the benefits of these activities, as they help create a supportive and encouraging environment. If I could relive the experience, I would participate more actively, perhaps by volunteering to help in future devotions.


Because of this activity, I am more mindful of the importance of spiritual growth and community in maintaining balance and positivity in life.
