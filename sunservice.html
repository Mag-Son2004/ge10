What?

I joined an evening Sunday church service with my friends at the Silliman Church on August 31. The service was vibrant and filled with people, creating a lively and uplifting atmosphere. The pastor delivered a heartfelt and inspiring message that resonated deeply with the congregation. The service included worship songs, prayers, and a sermon that emphasized faith, hope, and community.

So What?

Attending the service reminded me of the power of faith and how it brings people together. I initially expected a quiet and routine service, but I was pleasantly surprised by the energy and passion of the worship and the depth of the pastor’s message. One particular saying from the pastor about finding strength in challenges left a strong impression on me. The most satisfying part of the experience was the sense of unity and shared purpose among everyone present.

Now What?

This experience encouraged me to reflect on my spiritual journey and the importance of sharing it with others. I realized how attending church can provide comfort and motivation, especially when surrounded by friends and a supportive community. Moving forward, I plan to attend services more regularly and invite others to join as well. The benefits of such activities are clear—they foster a sense of belonging and provide guidance for daily life. If I could relive the experience, I would arrive earlier to fully immerse myself in the worship.


Because of this activity, I am more inspired to nurture my faith and value the sense of community that comes from worshiping together.
