What?

I attended SilliMove, a community charity event held at the Silliman University Amphitheatre on October 2 afternoon, where Ang Sandigan proudly participated and organized the event. The event celebrated inclusivity and aimed to support the deaf community by promoting awareness and raising funds for their needs. As an audience member, my role was to watch and cheer for the performers while appreciating the vibrant energy and message of unity the event conveyed. The Sandigan, along with other groups, performed dynamic dance routines, while organizers and volunteers ensured the event’s smooth flow.

So What?

Watching SilliMove deepened my understanding of how art, like dance, can foster inclusivity and bring people together for a meaningful cause. Initially, I expected the event to be just a typical showcase of talent, but I was moved by how each performance highlighted themes of diversity and empowerment, making it more impactful than I imagined. One performer from The Sandigan stood out to me with their expressive routine that beautifully blended movement and emotion, leaving a strong impression of dedication and inclusivity. The most satisfying part of the experience was seeing the audience, including myself, connect with the message and support the deaf community.

Now What?

This experience made me more aware of the importance of inclusivity and how events like SilliMove can break barriers while creating a sense of community. I’ve learned that supporting such initiatives, even as a viewer, contributes to amplifying their impact. Moving forward, I plan to engage more actively in similar events and advocate for inclusivity in my community. The benefits of SilliMove were clear—it raised awareness, fostered unity, and celebrated the beauty of diversity. If I could attend again, I would invite more friends to join and help promote the cause further.


Because of this activity, I am more inspired to support inclusive initiatives and celebrate the diversity that unites us all.
