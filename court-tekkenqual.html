What?

On November 2, I attended the Tekken Tournament: Qualifying Stage organized by the E-Sport Negrense League at the Dumaguete Convention Center. The event featured an updated version of the game with better graphics, new mechanics, and newly introduced characters, offering a fresh yet familiar experience. As a spectator, cheering on my friend, who joined the event for both competition and fun.

So What?

Watching my friend participate was exciting, especially as he almost reached the top 8. The other players showcased incredible skill, strategy, and competitiveness, making the event even more engaging. It was impressive to see how dedicated and strategic the participants were in mastering the game. The event highlighted the value of practice, focus, and adaptability in competitive gaming.

Now What?

This experience deepened my appreciation for e-sports and the effort players invest in improving their skills. It also inspired me to try playing Tekken with my friends casually or in preparation for future tournaments. If I could attend another event like this, I would actively cheer and even consider participating myself.


Because of this activity, I am more interested in e-sports and motivated to explore competitive gaming.
