What?

I attended an event titled “Balik Talent Lecture: Big Data and IoT: Why They Matter in Innovation” on August 20 at Alfredo T. Ang Hall (ATA Hall). The session, organized by the College of Computer Studies, lasted for about from 8:30AM to 11:30 AM and the lecture aimed to educate students about the importance of Big Data and IoT in driving innovation. The speaker, Mr. Abraham Johannes T. Tourbier, shared valuable insights about these technologies and their relevance to future opportunities. My role was as an audience member, listening and learning, while my friend and other students joined in, some of whom participated by asking questions during the Q&A session.

So What?

I gained a better understanding of how Big Data and IoT are shaping technological advancements and learned how these tools are applied in real-world scenarios. Initially, I expected a purely technical lecture, but I was surprised by the practical examples and career advice shared by the speaker, which made the discussion more relatable. Mr. Tourbier made a strong impression with his ability to simplify complex topics and his passion for innovation. The most satisfying part was realizing how these technologies can influence future innovations, though I found some technical jargon a bit challenging. This session highlighted the value of curiosity and staying updated with industry trends.

Now What?

This experience broadened my perspective on the importance of Big Data and IoT in the tech field and motivated me to explore these topics further. I plan to apply this knowledge by researching more about these technologies and incorporating them into future projects. Moving forward, I hope to attend similar events and actively participate in discussions. I see the benefits of events like this because they prepare us for future challenges and opportunities. If I could attend this lecture again, I would prepare questions to engage more with the speaker and deepen my understanding.


Because of this activity, I am more inspired to learn about emerging technologies and their impact on innovation.
