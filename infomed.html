What?

I attended the event All Things Infomedia on December 4 at Silliman University Guy Hall, organized by the SUSG Infomedia Committee. The session focused on how to create pubmats, posters, digital art, and lessons on photography. The speaker, Mr. Urich Calumpang, shared his experience and knowledge as a photographer. My role was as an audience member, actively listening and learning, while my friends were also there to participate and observe the session.

So What?

I learned new skills in creating digital art and pubmats, but what intrigued me most was the photography lesson. I expected a general talk, but I was surprised by how detailed and hands-on the photography discussion was. Mr. Calumpang left a strong impression on me with his passion for the craft. He taught us that everything can be a subject in photography and emphasized effort with his advice: “If you don’t sweat, get tired, and smell, you are not doing it right.” I liked how inspiring and engaging his talk was, and I found it satisfying to learn about photography techniques and creative tips.

Now What?

This experience changed the way I view photography—it’s not just about taking pictures but about capturing meaning and effort in every shot. I now feel inspired to pursue photography as a hobby and practice the skills I learned. Moving forward, I want to participate in similar workshops and continue improving my creative skills. I see the benefit of this activity because it encourages creativity and effort. If I could attend again, I would bring a camera and apply the lessons in real-time.


Because of this activity, I am more motivated to explore photography and become more creative in my work.
