What?

I attended an industry lecture titled “Designing Intuitive User Experiences: Key UI/UX Strategies” on August 22. The event was organized by the College of Computer Studies and held at Alfredo T. Ang Hall (ATA Hall), lasting approximately from 8am to 11:30am. It was attended by my Computer Studies classmates and facilitated by a young Software Developer and a UI/UX Designer. The session aimed to teach us about effective strategies for designing user-friendly and intuitive experiences. As an audience I actively listen and take notes as an audience member, while the speakers led the discussion and my classmates participated by asking questions and engaging with the speakers.

So What?

I learned valuable concepts about intuitive design and how collaboration between developers and designers enhances user experience. Initially, I expected a formal and technical lecture, but I was surprised by how engaging and interactive the session was. The UI/UX Designer made a strong positive impression on me by highlighting the importance of empathy in designing user-friendly systems. The most satisfying part of the lecture was the way it simplified complex ideas, making them easy to understand, though some advanced technical terms were still a bit challenging. This activity showed me the value of creativity and user-centered thinking in software development.

Now What?

This lecture inspired me to explore UI/UX design further and apply what I learned to my future projects. It also reminded me of the importance of balancing creativity with technical skills in software development. I plan to continue learning about UI/UX by attending similar events and working with my classmates on design projects. This activity clearly demonstrated the benefits of industry lectures, as they offer both practical knowledge and inspiration. If I could attend this event again, I would prepare more questions to engage more actively with the speakers and maximize the learning experience.


Because of this activity, I am more motivated to learn about UI/UX design and its impact on creating better software experiences.
