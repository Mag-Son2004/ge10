What?

I attended the Mental Health Literacy Workshop: “Empowering Minds & Safe Space” on October 16 at Alfredo T. Ang Hall (ATA Hall). The event was organized by the Silliman University Bachelor of Library and Information Systems Society and aimed to educate participants about mental health, how to support individuals facing mental health issues, and how to create safe spaces. As an audience, listening to the lecture and learning from the speakers. I attended with my friends, and the organizers facilitated the session, ensuring it was informative and engaging.

So What?

Through this workshop, I learned the importance of understanding and addressing mental health issues. Initially, I thought the session would focus only on theory, but I was surprised by the practical advice shared about helping those struggling with mental health challenges. The speakers made a strong impression on me, especially with their emphasis on creating safe spaces for others. I appreciated how clear and relatable their examples were. The most satisfying part was gaining knowledge that is relevant and helpful in today’s generation, as mental health is often overlooked but very important.

Now What?

This experience changed how I view mental health, making me more aware of how I can support others. I learned the importance of listening, understanding, and providing a safe space for people dealing with these issues. Moving forward, I plan to apply what I learned by being more sensitive to the needs of my friends and family and spreading awareness about mental health. I see the benefits of workshops like this because they equip people with knowledge that can help make a positive difference. If I could attend this event again, I would ask more questions to deepen my understanding.


Because of this activity, I am more informed and empowered to promote mental health awareness and create safe spaces for others.
