What?

I attended an evening midweek prayer service alone at the Silliman Church on September 11. The service was quiet and relaxing, with only a small group of people present, creating an intimate and peaceful atmosphere. The pastor shared a thoughtful message focused on the prelude and processional, emphasizing their spiritual significance and how they prepare the heart and mind for worship.

So What?

The service gave me a chance to reflect and find peace amidst a busy week. I initially expected it to feel a bit lonely attending alone, but the serene environment and meaningful sermon made it a calming and personal experience. The pastor’s insights on the importance of preparation in worship left a strong impression on me, reminding me of the value of intentionality in spiritual practices. The most fulfilling part was being able to sit quietly, listen, and feel a sense of connection to my faith in the stillness of the moment.

Now What?

This experience taught me the importance of taking time for personal reflection and prayer, even outside of larger gatherings. It helped me appreciate the quiet moments in worship and how they can deepen one’s connection with God. Moving forward, I plan to attend midweek prayer services more often, as they provide a much-needed pause in my routine. If I could do something differently, I would engage more by bringing a journal to write down the pastor’s insights for deeper reflection later.


Because of this activity, I am more aware of the importance of quiet reflection and preparation in nurturing my spiritual life
