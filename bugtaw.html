What?

I attended the Bugtaw Anniversary event on December 10 organized by the place itself, celebrating the 4th anniversary of Bugtaw through music. The event featured local artists and bands from Negros Oriental and was filled with people enjoying the performances. My role was as a guest, invited to eat and watch, while the bands and artists showcased their talents in various music styles and genres. I also met some old friends and familiar faces, making the event even more memorable.

So What?

The event widened my perspective on music culture as I noticed how each band had its own unique style, genre, and instruments. It was fascinating to see the diversity in their performances. I wasn’t just there for the music but also enjoyed watching the audience connect with the energy of the event. The lively atmosphere and the passion of the performers left a strong impression on me, making the experience both entertaining and inspiring.

Now What?

This experience gave me a deeper appreciation for local music and the vibrant culture surrounding it. Moving forward, I’d like to explore more local events like this and support artists in their craft. The event also taught me the value of community and how music can bring people together. If I could attend again, I’d spend more time talking to the artists and learning about their creative process.


Because of this activity, I am more inspired to explore and appreciate the rich and diverse music culture in my community.
