What?

My friends and I attended Island Madness 3 on October 12, a car show event held at Freedom Park in Dumaguete on Buglasan Festival. This event, organized by the Provincial Government of Negros Oriental, was a celebration of car culture featuring vehicles from different brands, styles, and places. As an audience, we admired the cars on display, while car enthusiasts and participants showcased their vehicles and shared their passion for automotive culture.

So What?

The event gave me a deeper understanding of how cars are not just machines but also cultural symbols that reflect creativity, aesthetics, and innovation. I was especially amazed to see the Mitsubishi Lancer Evo 4, my favorite Japanese car, and felt so happy to take photos of it. My expectations were exceeded because I realized how events like this bring people together to celebrate shared interests. The most satisfying part was seeing people of different ages and backgrounds enjoying the vibrant car culture in such a lively atmosphere.

Now What?

This experience made me appreciate how car shows can highlight cultural diversity through different vehicle styles and designs. It also inspired me to learn more about automotive culture and history. I now see the value of attending events like this because they promote creativity, community, and appreciation for cars as cultural icons. If I could attend a similar event again, I would take more time to engage with the car owners and learn about their stories.


Because of this activity, I am more aware of how car culture brings people together and celebrates creativity and shared passion.
