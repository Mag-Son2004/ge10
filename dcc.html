What?

On November 15, I was part of the lead organizing team for the CYF (Christian Youth Fellowship) a Friday Night Bible Study, held at our local church. The event lasted for one evening and focused on fostering spiritual growth among the youth. Our role was to handle the technical aspects, such as setting up audio-visual equipment ,adjusting the light ,setting-up the presentations and ensuring smooth transitions during the program. Other organizers were responsible for coordinating the activities, leading discussions, and managing the fellowship. The session aimed to deepen our understanding of faith while encouraging fellowship among attendees.

So What?

Through this experience, I enhanced my technical and organizational skills, which were critical to ensuring the event’s success. Initially, I expected my role to be straightforward, but I was surprised by the level of teamwork required and how much I learned from the process. A fellow organizer who mentored me made a positive impression with their patience and problem-solving skills, which inspired me to stay calm under pressure. The most satisfying part of the activity was seeing how the technical setup contributed to a meaningful and smooth-flowing session for everyone.

Now What?

This experience has broadened my perspective on collaboration and the importance of small contributions in achieving larger goals. I learned to value teamwork and how technical work can play a vital role in community-building. Moving forward, I plan to stay involved with the group by volunteering for future events and sharing what I’ve learned with others. The benefits of this activity are clear, as it brought people together while allowing me to grow personally. If I could do it again, I would focus more on planning ahead to anticipate technical challenges.


Because of this activity, I am more confident in my ability to contribute more to our church events and inspired to continue serving in meaningful ways.
