What?

I participated as a logistics officer during the 2024 Youngblood Initiative, a blood donation event organized by the Department of Science and Technology Scholars’ Association in Silliman University (DOST SU). The event was held on [insert date] at Silliman University ,Oriental Hall and aimed to encourage students and the community to donate blood, promoting health awareness and saving lives. My role involved managing supplies, setting up the venue, and coordinating with volunteers and medical staff to ensure the smooth flow of operations. The other team members handled promotions, donor registration, and medical checks.

So What?

This activity taught me how to coordinate effectively under pressure and manage logistics efficiently. Initially, I expected my tasks to be straightforward, but I soon realized the complexity of ensuring everything ran smoothly. It surprised me how crucial teamwork and communication were to the event’s success. One of the medical staff made a strong impression on me by patiently guiding first-time donors, reminding me of the importance of empathy in service. The most satisfying part was seeing donors leave with smiles, knowing they contributed to saving lives.

Now What?

This experience broadened my perspective on the importance of community service and teamwork. I learned that small efforts, like organizing logistics, contribute significantly to larger causes. Moving forward, I plan to remain active in DOST SU events, applying the organizational skills I gained to future projects. I now see the immense benefits of participating in such initiatives, as they not only help others but also foster personal growth. If I could do the project again, I would focus more on enhancing donor comfort and creating a more engaging atmosphere.


Because of this activity, I am more aware of the value of teamwork and the impact of collective efforts in making a difference.
