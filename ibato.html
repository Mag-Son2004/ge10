What?

I participated in the “Ding Ibato! : Dodgeball for a Cause” event organized by the Department of Science and Technology Scholars’ Association in Silliman University (DOST SU). The event took place on November 11 afternoon at Silliman University ,Ravello Field and its goal was to raise funds for the Dumaguete Animal Sanctuary. My friends and I formed a team and competed in a dodgeball tournament. The other teams were also made up of DOST SU members, and they played with the same goal of supporting the sanctuary. Our role was to play our best and have fun, while the organizers managed the event details.

So What?

During the event, I learned how to work together better as a team, especially in a competitive setting. Initially, I thought the event would be casual, but I soon realized how much strategy and coordination were required. I was surprised by how intense the competition became, and how everyone, including the spectators, showed so much enthusiasm for the cause. One teammate, who was very competitive, left a strong impression on me as they motivated the rest of us to give our best. The most satisfying part was seeing how much support there was for the animal sanctuary, even though we didn’t finish in 3rd place.

Now What?

This experience made me realize how much fun it can be to participate in events that support a good cause. I learned that even small actions, like playing in a charity tournament, can contribute to something bigger. I plan to stay involved with DOST SU and continue participating in future events, as they combine fun with helping others. I definitely see the benefits of doing activities like this because they promote teamwork and raise awareness for important causes. If I were to do this again, I would focus more on improving our team coordination.


Because of this activity, I am more motivated to take part in events that support causes I care about.
