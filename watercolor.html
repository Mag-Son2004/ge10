What?

I attended an online webinar titled “Watercolor Painting for Your Self-Care and Mental Health” on the evening of September 12, organized by Artillery Philippines in partnership with Silliman University. The session aimed to promote mental well-being through creative expression using watercolor painting. My role as a participant was to actively engage by learning the basics of watercolor techniques and understanding how art supports self-care and mental health. The instructor led the webinar, demonstrating painting techniques and sharing insights, while other participants painted along and shared their creations, fostering a supportive and interactive environment.

So What?

This webinar taught me the therapeutic benefits of creative activities like watercolor painting and their connection to mental health. I initially expected it to focus only on painting techniques, but I was pleasantly surprised by the emphasis on self-expression and mindfulness. Seeing other participants proudly share their artwork during the session left a strong impression on me, highlighting how art can connect and inspire people. The most satisfying part of the webinar was the calming and encouraging atmosphere, which allowed me to relax and explore painting without fear of judgment.

Now What?

This experience helped me appreciate the importance of incorporating creative outlets into my self-care routine. I learned that painting doesn’t have to be perfect to be meaningful and that the process itself is a source of healing and joy. Moving forward, I plan to continue exploring watercolor painting as a personal hobby and seek out similar webinars to deepen my skills. I see the benefits of activities like this because they support individual well-being and foster community connections. If I could attend a similar event again, I would participate more actively by painting along and sharing my work with the group.


Because of this activity, I am more aware of the importance of self-care and how creative expression can positively impact mental health.
